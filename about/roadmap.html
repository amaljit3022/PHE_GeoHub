PHED Assam – GIS & Remote Sensing Roadmap

Vision

To institutionalize space and GIS-based solutions in all facets of water supply, groundwater management, climate resilience, and monitoring in rural Assam.

Short-Term Goals (Next 2 Years)

  • Greywater management using GIS: site suitability for soak pits and DEWATS
  • Surface source sustainability via river course and erosion monitoring
  • Flood risk mapping and integration of hazard layers in asset design

Medium-Term Goals (2–5 Years)

  • Groundwater recharge and depletion mapping using NISAR and satellite datasets
  • Aquifer mapping and flow modeling using lithology and backscatter data
  • IoT and satellite-enabled real-time field monitoring and flow sensing
  • Solar feasibility mapping for sustainable pump house energy
  • Water Quality Index prediction using environmental indicators and satellite indices
  • Establishment of GIS-based Command Centers at State HQ

Long-Term Goals

  • Integration of SatNav & SatCom for end-to-end asset monitoring and data streaming
  • AI-assisted predictive modeling of groundwater and contamination patterns
  • Cross-sectoral planning integration with Gati Shakti Portal and urban WASH systems

Capacity Building & Infrastructure

  • Training field engineers in GEE, Python, and QGIS
  • Establishing satellite imagery pipelines using AWS/GEE/Bhuvan
  • Deploying ArcGIS/ENVI/ERDAS software on high-performance machines

Expected Benefits

Improved transparency, accurate water resource mapping, data-driven decision making, better disaster resilience, and efficient scheme implementation in rural Assam under PHED.
