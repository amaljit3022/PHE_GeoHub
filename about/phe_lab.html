PHE GIS & Hydrogeology Lab

About the Lab

The PHE GIS & Hydrogeology Lab, established within the Office of the Mission Director, JJM Assam, serves as the technical nucleus for all geospatial and remote sensing initiatives under the department. Originally constituted with two dedicated GIS Experts, the lab has now evolved into a larger team.

The lab currently supports a wide range of GIS-based planning, monitoring, and modeling activities under major programs like Jal Jeevan Mission and Swachh Bharat Mission, along with cross-sectoral planning through the PM Gati Shakti Portal.

Team Structure

  • GIS Expert: Sri Samsul Alam (Team Lead)
  • Technical Officers – GIS: Sri Sanghita Sarma, Sri Arnab Gogoi, Sri Dipannita Haloi, Sri Rahul Saikia, Sri Sunayana Goswami
  • Technical Officers – Hydrogeology: Sri Marshal Pegu, Sri Jayashree Dutta
  • Engineering Support: AE, AEE, SE, ACE as per technical assignments

Core Functions

  • GIS-based mapping and monitoring of pipeline infrastructure
  • Groundwater zone mapping and hydrological modeling
  • Asset geotagging using GPS-enabled mobile tools
  • Validation of surface and groundwater sources using satellite data
  • Integration of GIS layers with PM Gati Shakti Portal via BISAG-N
  • Support to Jal Shakti Abhiyan (CTR) and WQ Index risk mapping

Software and Tools

The lab operates with licensed versions of ArcGIS (Advanced and Standard), QGIS, Google Earth Engine, and remote sensing platforms supported by NRSC, ISRO, and Bhuvan. Emphasis is also given to open-source solutions and field training of district-level engineers.

Future Vision

The lab aims to expand its technical capacity toward climate resilience, IoT integration, water quality modeling, flood mapping, and solar feasibility assessment using AI and satellite analytics.
